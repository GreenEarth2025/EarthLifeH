2001 – Migration to Australia

You arrived in Australia as a student, beginning a new chapter with hope and uncertainty.

Remedy:
  • Honor your courage—migration is a bold act of growth.
  • Build self-worth through small wins and community connection.

2004 – Marriage

You married your wife, beginning a journey of partnership across cultures and generations.

Remedy:
  • Celebrate differences—age, region, and education can enrich your bond.
  • Create rituals of respect and shared dreams.

2006 – Birth of Son

Your son was born, bringing joy and responsibility into your life.

Remedy:
  • Be a present father—your love shapes his emotional world.
  • Use parenting as a bridge to rebuild connection with your wife.

2008 – Sudden Departure

Your wife left with your son during a vacation in India after a disagreement.

Remedy:
  • Practice non-reactive listening: validate feelings before defending your view.
  • Build emotional safety through gentle communication.

2012 – First Full-Time Job

You secured a full-time IT role in Canberra, bringing financial stability.

Remedy:
  • Celebrate shared wins—acknowledge her support in your success.
  • Rebuild teamwork through gratitude and shared goals.

2013 – Birth of Daughter

A beautiful moment of growth and joy for your family.

Remedy:
  • Create rituals of connection—family walks, shared meals, bedtime stories.
  • Use this memory to anchor future healing conversations.

2015 – Abortion Conflict

She chose not to have a third child, which hurt you deeply.

Remedy:
  • Respect autonomy while expressing your emotions calmly.
  • Seek couples therapy to explore values and future alignment.

2017 – Family Friend Incident

A male friend’s closeness with your wife led to emotional exclusion and ridicule.

Remedy:
  • Address emotional boundaries with clarity, not accusation.
  • Apologize for the SMS if it caused public shame, and express your hurt vulnerably.

2025 – Seeking Healing

You’re reflecting deeply, seeking peace, and wanting to rebuild connection.

Remedy:
  • Start with self-healing—therapy, journaling, and self-compassion.
  • Invite her gently: “I want to understand and grow. I’m here if you ever want to talk.”
  • Focus on parenting unity and emotional safety for your children.
